Why Anchored exists

An app that gives you your time back.

Most screen-time tools shame you, gamify you, or sell you premium tiers to keep you engaged. We thought that was strange — an app supposed to give you your time back, asking for more of it.

The premise

Doomscrolling is one of the most quietly destructive habits of the smartphone era. People lose hours they meant to spend reading, sleeping, cooking, talking. Most of us know it. Most of us still do it.

The tools meant to help us do less of it tend to fall into one of three traps. Some lock you out so completely that you uninstall them by week two. Some turn the whole thing into a game, with streaks and badges, which only adds another layer of compulsion on top of the original one. And some quietly harvest your habits to sell to advertisers.

Anchored takes a different approach.

Three principles

Three ideas guide every decision we make, from the copy on a button to the structure of a feature. They are the rails the product runs on.

Friction, not punishment

A short pause to reflect is more honest than an absolute lockout. When you hit a block, Anchored does not pretend the option to bypass does not exist. It just makes you stop and write a sentence about why first. Adults can decide for themselves. The pause is the point.

Data without verdict

We show your usage as a number and a trend, not a grade. There are no streaks unless you turn them on. The bar chart never highlights "worst" days. It just shows the shape of your week, calmly drawn, so you can decide what to do with it.

Calm by default

Anchored does not send notifications begging you to come back. There are no badges to unlock, no levels to climb, no "Day 7 streak!" celebrations. The best version of this app is the one you forget is running.

An older idea

The closest precedent for what we are trying to build is something Mark Weiser, the father of ubiquitous computing, wrote in 1991 about the future of technology:

The most profound technologies are those that disappear. They weave themselves into the fabric of everyday life until they are indistinguishable from it. — Mark Weiser, 1991

Most of the apps on your phone were not designed this way. They were designed to be visible, sticky, and noisy. Anchored is designed to be the opposite: quiet, restrained, and slightly forgettable. If we have done our job well, you will think about us less, not more.

Who this is for

Anchored is built for people who already know they want to use their phones less, and who are tired of being shouted at about it. If you are looking for a coach, an accountability partner, or a community, this is not the right tool. If you want a quiet, calm, well-built thing that helps you do less of something you have already decided to do less of, you are in the right place.

Anchored is built independently. It has no investors, takes no advertising, and will never be free at the cost of your attention. If we ever charge for it, we will say so, plainly.
