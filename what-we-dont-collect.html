What we don’t collect

A short list. That’s the point.

Most apps publish a long privacy policy that hides what they collect. We thought it was simpler to publish what we don’t.

Anchored is built to need almost nothing about you. Below is the full list of things we do not collect, do not store, and do not share. If something is not on this list, it does not exist in our systems.

Your scrolling content

What you watch, what you like, who you follow. Anchored has no idea, and no way to find out.

Your reflections

The text you write to bypass a block stays on your device. We do not see it, store it, or analyse it.

Identifiable analytics

No third-party trackers. No fingerprinting. No "anonymous" telemetry that can be re-identified later.

Your contacts or calendar

Anchored does not request access to either. There is no feature that needs them.

Your location

We do not ask for it, do not record it, and do not use it for any purpose, including geofenced blocks.

Your messages

Even when blocking apps, Anchored never reads message content. It just controls what opens and when.

What about the small amount we do touch?

Anchored does need to know which apps you have asked it to block, and at what times. This information lives on your device. If you choose to create an optional account to sync across devices, that schedule data is end-to-end encrypted before it leaves your phone.

Aggregate screen-time numbers, the kind you see in the Insights tab, come from the operating system’s public Screen Time API. Anchored reads those values to show you, but does not transmit them anywhere.

If we ever change this

If we ever decide to collect anything new, we will say so on this page, in clear language, before doing it. We will also ask before turning it on. The default will always be off.

For the legal language version, see the privacy policy.
