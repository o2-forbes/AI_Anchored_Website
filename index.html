Three principles

A different kind
of intervention.

Most screen-time tools shame you, gamify you, or sell you premium tiers to keep you engaged. Anchored does none of those things.

01
Friction, not punishment.

A short pause to reflect is more honest than an absolute lockout. Adults can decide for themselves. When you hit a block, Anchored asks why before letting you bypass. The path is always available; it just takes a moment of intention.

02
Data without verdict.

See your time as a trend, never a grade. No streaks unless you turn them on. No worst-offender lists. No celebratory animations. Just the shape of your week, calmly drawn so you can decide what to do with it.

03
Calm by default.

The best version of this app is the one you forget is running. No notifications begging you to come back. No badges, no level-ups. Anchored does its job in the background, and steps aside the moment it's no longer useful.

How it works

Set up in
under a minute.

Anchored asks for nothing more than your attention for 60 seconds, and a single intention.

Pick what you'd like to gain.

Sleep better. Focus when working. Spend more time on hobbies. Feel calmer overall. One choice, framed as a gain, not a loss. Change it whenever.

Schedule blocks around your real life.

A bedtime window. Work hours. Custom evenings. Choose messaging-only or fully blocked. Toggle each schedule on or off in a tap.

Reflect, don't rage-quit.

When a block fires, you can return to your home screen, or write a short reflection to unblock for 15 minutes. Either path is fine.
